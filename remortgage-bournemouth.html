Remortgage Advice in Bournemouth

Fixed rate ending? Don't drift onto your lender's standard variable rate. We'll find your genuinely best next deal.

You can lock in a new mortgage rate up to six months before your current fixed deal ends — and if you do nothing, you'll roll onto your lender's Standard Variable Rate, which is almost always far more expensive. NexMoney compares hundreds of lenders (including your current one) and handles the whole switch. Many of our remortgages are arranged with no broker fee, and lots of deals include free valuation and legal work.

Remortgage vs product transfer — we check both

Your existing lender will offer you a product transfer: a new rate with minimal paperwork. Sometimes that's the right answer — it's fast and needs no re-underwriting. But lenders reserve their sharpest pricing for new customers, so a full remortgage to another lender frequently wins once fees are accounted for. As whole-of-market brokers we compare your lender's retention deals against the open market and recommend whichever leaves you better off — we're not tied to either outcome.

The right time to act

Start around six months out. We secure a new deal early as your safety net; if rates drop before your old deal expires, we re-check the market and switch you to the cheaper option — most lenders allow this right up to completion. Leaving it to the final month narrows your options and risks a spell on the SVR while paperwork catches up.

Release equity while you switch

A remortgage is also the natural moment to raise capital — for an extension or renovation, a deposit on a second property or buy-to-let, or to consolidate more expensive debt. Lenders will assess affordability on the new, higher balance, and rolling short-term debt into a 25-year mortgage can cost more overall even at a lower rate — we'll always show you the total cost both ways before you decide.

Remortgage FAQs

Your questions, answered

When should I start my remortgage?

Around six months before your current fix ends. You can lock a rate early as insurance, and if rates fall before completion we can usually move you to the cheaper deal.

What happens if I do nothing when my fix ends?

You roll onto your lender's Standard Variable Rate (SVR) — almost always significantly more expensive than a new fixed or tracker deal.

Remortgage or product transfer — which is better?

It depends. A product transfer is faster with less paperwork; a full remortgage often unlocks better pricing or extra borrowing. We compare both, including all fees, and recommend whichever genuinely costs less.

Does remortgaging cost anything?

Many deals include free valuation and legal work, and NexMoney arranges many remortgages with no broker fee. Where fees apply, we build them into the true-cost comparison upfront.

Can I release equity when I remortgage?

Yes — for home improvements, a second-property deposit, or debt consolidation. We'll show the full-term cost implications before you commit.
