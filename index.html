Common Questions

Mortgage Questions
Answered

Not sure where to start? Here are the questions we're asked most often — or visit our full FAQ page for more.

How much can I borrow for a mortgage?

Most lenders will let you borrow between 4 and 4.5 times your annual income, though some will go up to 5 or 5.5 times for professionals and higher earners. Joint applicants can combine incomes. Lenders also weigh up your outgoings, debts, and credit history. Use our mortgage calculator for a quick estimate.

What deposit do I need to buy a house?

The minimum deposit is 5% of the property's value for most residential mortgages. A 10% or larger deposit unlocks better rates. First-time buyers can use the Lifetime ISA or Shared Ownership to boost their savings. Buy-to-let investors typically need at least 25%.

Can I get a mortgage with bad credit?

Yes — it depends on the type and age of the credit issue. Minor blemishes like a missed payment often have little impact, especially after three years. More serious issues like CCJs or IVAs require specialist lenders. NexMoney has experience placing clients with adverse credit and knows which lenders to approach.

What is a whole-of-market mortgage broker?

A whole-of-market broker searches hundreds of lenders — not just a limited panel — to find the most competitive deal for your circumstances. NexMoney is whole-of-market, which means we're not tied to any lender, and our advice is always in your best interest.

How does a mortgage broker get paid?

Brokers earn a commission from the lender (called a procuration fee) when your mortgage completes, and may charge you a broker fee. NexMoney's typical fee is £375, always disclosed upfront. On many remortgages there is no fee to you at all. We're fully transparent about our fees before you commit.
