First Time Buyer Mortgages in Bournemouth

Everything you need to get from "how do I even start?" to keys in hand — with a whole-of-market broker on your side.

A first-time buyer in Bournemouth typically needs a deposit of at least 5% and can borrow around 4 to 4.5 times their annual income — sometimes more. As whole-of-market brokers, NexMoney compares hundreds of lenders (not just the high street) to find the deal that fits your deposit, income and credit history, and we handle the entire application for you. Our advice is FCA-regulated and your first consultation is always free.

How much deposit do you really need?

The headline answer is 5% of the purchase price — but the bigger your deposit, the better your rate. Lenders price mortgages in loan-to-value (LTV) bands, so moving from a 95% to a 90% LTV mortgage can cut your monthly payment meaningfully. Family can help too: many lenders accept gifted deposits from parents or grandparents, and some offer family-assist products where a relative's savings or property act as security instead of a cash deposit.

Schemes that get you there faster

Several schemes exist specifically for first-time buyers. The Lifetime ISA adds a 25% government bonus to up to £4,000 of savings a year, provided you've held the account for at least 12 months before buying. Shared ownership lets you buy a 25–75% share of a home and pay subsidised rent on the rest — a popular route into Bournemouth's new-build developments. First Homes offers discounted new-builds to eligible local buyers and key workers. We'll tell you which schemes you actually qualify for and whether they beat a standard 95% mortgage in your case — the answer isn't always obvious.

Buying your first home in Bournemouth

Bournemouth remains one of the more accessible coastal markets on the South Coast. Areas like Winton, Charminster, Moordown and Boscombe are long-standing first-time buyer favourites, with flats and terraced homes well below the town's average price, while Kinson and West Howe offer some of the lowest entry points in the conurbation. As a Bournemouth-based broker we know which local property types certain lenders are cautious about — ex-local-authority flats, holiday-let-heavy blocks, non-standard construction — and which lenders take a pragmatic view.

What the process looks like

First, a free chat about your income, deposit and goals — no credit check, no obligation. We then get you a decision in principle (usually within 24 hours) so you can make offers with confidence. Once your offer on a property is accepted, we submit the full application, chase the lender and valuer, and keep your solicitor moving. Most first purchases complete within 8–12 weeks of the offer being accepted, and we stay with you until the day you get the keys — and again when your first fixed rate ends.

First Time Buyer FAQs

Your questions, answered

How much deposit does a first-time buyer need in Bournemouth?

The minimum is 5% of the property's value for most residential mortgages. A 10% or larger deposit unlocks better rates. Gifted deposits from family are widely accepted, and a Lifetime ISA adds a 25% government bonus to your savings.

How much can a first-time buyer borrow?

Most lenders offer 4 to 4.5 times your annual income, with some stretching to 5 or 5.5 times for higher earners and certain professions. Joint applicants can combine incomes. Use our calculator for a quick estimate.

What schemes help first-time buyers?

The Lifetime ISA, shared ownership, First Homes, and family-assist or guarantor mortgages. Which one suits you depends on your savings, income and the property you want — we'll walk you through the numbers for each.

How long does a first-time buyer mortgage take?

A decision in principle usually takes under 24 hours, a full mortgage offer 2–4 weeks, and the whole purchase commonly completes in 8–12 weeks.

Do first-time buyers pay stamp duty?

First-time buyers get stamp duty relief on properties up to a government-set threshold. Thresholds change, so we confirm the current position for your specific purchase as part of our advice.
